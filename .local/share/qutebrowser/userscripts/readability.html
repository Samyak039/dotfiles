Don’t miss the 2021-01 update to my blogger workflow where many new tools were added and an important one was kicked out for privacy protection reasons.

It’s been a while since I provided an update to my blogger workflow. The last time was back in September of 2020. It’s time for some tweaks.

The number of services I pay for has increased. Now, I’m paying for IFTTT and Buffer. IFTTT has improved its offerings this year and started to offer a paid tier. I recently added an automation where new posts from my Micro.blog RSS feed will get saved to a Google Sheet document. It makes it a lot easier to find previous post references when I’m working on my Numeric Ctizien Introspection newsletter.

The reason I’m paying for Buffer is the added features I get in return. I get more channels and better post management. All of my blog posts are now queued to Buffer. From there, I can choose when is my blog post will be reposted. Sometimes, I may elect to not repost some of my work. Another use of Buffer is when there is an Apple event coming up. I will schedule tweets in advance. On the day of the event, I can focus on the event itself. It’s working fine for me this way.

The other addition is in fact a return of an old buddy: Reeder 5, the venerable RSS feed reader. I’m still depending on News Explorer (read my review here). The reason I’m using Reeder 5 is the Read Later feature where I put all my internet articles that I want to eventually read. It works great, supports tags and runs on all Apple platforms.

Probably on the way out is MarsEdit. It’s not that the app is bad, quite the contrary. What I’m looking for is to consolidate my writing apps. Ulysses is rumoured to introduce support for Micro.blog beginning of next year. If that’s the case, I’ll drop MarsEdit.

In the next few weeks, I’m looking forward to Apple finally approving my submission of this blog for publication on Apple News. They recently updated their review process and asked all content creators waiting to resubmit their articles and answer a questionnaire. We’ll see.
